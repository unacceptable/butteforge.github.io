# Purpose

The Butte Forge: Embedded Systems & AI is a student-led technology incubator
designed to complement and extend the Computer Science and Engineering
curriculum at Montana Tech through intensive, hands-on, project-driven work.

The purpose of The Butte Forge is to bridge the gap between theoretical
computer science and real-world engineering by focusing on embedded systems,
digital electronics, sensing, communication, and applied artificial
intelligence. Students work on practical problems that require integrating
hardware, software, and systems-level thinking - skills that are difficult
to fully develop within traditional lecture-based courses.

Rather than functioning as a typical student club, The Butte Forge operates
as an experiential learning environment where members engage in the full
lifecycle of engineering projects: problem definition, system design,
implementation, testing, iteration, and deployment.

## Objectives

By participating in The Butte Forge, students will:

- Develop strong systems-level thinking across hardware, software, and data
  domains
- Gain practical experience with embedded systems, sensors, communication
  protocols, and edge AI
- Learn how to design and deploy reliable solutions under real-world
  constraints
- Build leadership, collaboration, and project management skills in a
  technical setting
- Learn how to learn independently in a rapidly evolving technological
  landscape

The organization emphasizes technological agility rather than
platform-specific training, preparing students to adapt to emerging tools
and architectures throughout their careers.
